Photosynthesis Notes
Subject: Basic Science
Level: JSS2

What is Photosynthesis?
Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants make their own food.
Plants use sunlight to change water and carbon dioxide into food (a sugar called glucose).
Because plants can make their own food, we call them producers.

What Do Plants Need for Photosynthesis?
Plants need four main things to carry out photosynthesis:
1. Sunlight - this gives the energy for the process.
2. Water - the plant takes in water from the soil through its roots.
3. Carbon dioxide - a gas the plant takes in from the air through tiny holes in the leaves called stomata.
4. Chlorophyll - the green colour inside leaves that traps sunlight energy.

The Role of Chlorophyll
Chlorophyll is found in small green parts of the leaf cells called chloroplasts.
Chlorophyll traps energy from sunlight. Without chlorophyll, a plant cannot carry out photosynthesis.
This is why most leaves are green.

What Does Photosynthesis Produce?
Photosynthesis produces two things:
1. Glucose - a sugar that gives the plant energy and helps it grow.
2. Oxygen - a gas released into the air through the leaves.

The oxygen released by plants is the same oxygen that humans and animals breathe.
This is one reason why plants are so important to life on Earth.

Word Equation for Photosynthesis
Carbon dioxide + Water --(sunlight and chlorophyll)--> Glucose + Oxygen

Why Plants Are Called Producers
Plants are called producers because they produce (make) their own food.
Animals cannot make their own food, so they are called consumers.
Many animals depend on plants for food and oxygen. This makes plants the base of the food chain.

Key Terms
- Photosynthesis: the process by which green plants make their own food using sunlight.
- Chlorophyll: the green substance in leaves that traps sunlight.
- Stomata: tiny holes in leaves that let in carbon dioxide and let out oxygen.
- Glucose: the sugar (food) made during photosynthesis.
- Producer: a living thing that makes its own food.
